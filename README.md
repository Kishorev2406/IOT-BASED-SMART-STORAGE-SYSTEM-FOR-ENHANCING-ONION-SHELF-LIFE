The IoT-Based Smart Storage System for Enhancing Onion Shelf Life is an innovative agricultural monitoring solution developed to address the challenges associated with onion storage and post-harvest losses. Onions are highly sensitive to environmental factors such as temperature, humidity, and harmful gases released during decomposition. Traditional storage methods often rely on manual inspection and natural ventilation, which are inefficient in detecting unfavorable conditions at an early stage. To overcome these limitations, this project integrates modern IoT technology with environmental sensing and automated control mechanisms to ensure optimal storage conditions.

The system is built around the ESP32 microcontroller, which acts as the central processing unit and communicates with DHT11 temperature-humidity sensors and an MQ135 gas sensor. These sensors continuously monitor the storage environment and collect real-time data regarding temperature fluctuations, moisture levels, and spoilage-related gases such as ammonia and volatile organic compounds. The collected data is processed by the ESP32 and transmitted to the ThingsBoard cloud platform through Wi-Fi connectivity, allowing users to remotely access environmental information through dashboards, graphs, and historical data records.

A key feature of the system is its automated ventilation control mechanism. Whenever the monitored parameters exceed predefined safety thresholds, the ESP32 activates a relay module connected to an exhaust fan. The fan improves air circulation, removes excess humidity, and disperses harmful gases, thereby maintaining a stable environment suitable for long-term onion preservation. Once the environmental conditions return to safe levels, the system automatically switches off the fan, ensuring energy-efficient operation.

By combining sensor technology, cloud computing, real-time monitoring, and automatic control, the proposed system significantly reduces spoilage, extends onion shelf life, and minimizes economic losses for farmers and storage operators. The project promotes smart agriculture by providing a low-cost, scalable, and reliable solution that improves storage management, enhances food quality, and supports sustainable agricultural practices. Furthermore, the collected data can be used for future analysis and decision-making, enabling farmers to optimize storage conditions and improve overall productivity.
